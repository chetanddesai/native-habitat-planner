Native Habitat Gardening

A chickadee needs nine thousand caterpillars to raise one clutch of chicks. Those caterpillars can only eat the native plants they evolved with over millennia. When we filled our yards with lawns and ornamentals, we didn't just change the scenery — we broke the food web that every songbird, butterfly, and pollinator depends on. But here's what Tallamy wants us to see: our residential landscapes, taken together, are larger than every national park in America. The most important conservation decision you'll make isn't a vote or a donation. It's what you plant next.

Suggest a Plant

Know a native plant that should be included? Open a GitHub issue with the scientific name, region, and why it belongs.

Suggest a Region

Want to see your city represented? Open a GitHub issue with the city/state and either an iNaturalist place ID or bounding box coordinates.

Submit a Pull Request

Developers can fork the repo and use the add-plant or add-city skills to contribute new data. See the README for details.
